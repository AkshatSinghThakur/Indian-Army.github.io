The Gazhi Attack 1971

Mission Overview

After the Pulwama attack and airstrike, the tension between India and Pakistan is continuously increasing. Recently, Pakistan has accused India that there has been an intrusion into their maritime border from an Indian side. The increasing pressure in the maritime border of the two countries is not new. The Bollywood film 'Ghazi Attack' has been made on the story of one such war of 1971. The movie depicts the eye-to-eye battle between Indian's INS Vikrant and Pakistani submarine PNS Ghazi.


In the war of 1971, Pakistan knew that India's INS Vikrant is the factor to change the picture of the war. In this battle, Pakistan sent its Naval Submarine Ghazi to sink INS Vikrant, which is said to have been sunk on the intervening night of 3 and 4 December. PNS Ghazi-71 was the most hidden lethal weapon of Pakistan in the war. At that time India did not have a single submarine. In such a situation, stopping Ghazi was a challenging task. An even bigger challenge was Ghazi's psychological intimidation. If Ghazi had succeeded in his mission, it would have been a big defeat for India psychologically. Which would have affected the outcome of the 1971 war as well.


Ghazi had the ability to travel 20 thousand kilometers long. That's why Ghazi was quietly sent from Karachi to the Bay of Bengal between 14 November and 22 November just before the war of 71 started. But his real mission is to find and destroy the aircraft carrier Vikrant. The Indian Navy had come to know from signal interception that Ghazi is somewhere in the sea between Karachi to Bay of Bengal. This was bad news for Vikrant, who had left the port of Visakhapatnam for a maritime siege of East Pakistan.
